About Me

I’m an electrical engineering technology student at Purdue University’s main campus, graduating in December 2026. Before transferring into Engineering Technology, I spent two years in Electrical and Computer Engineering, which gave me a strong foundation in circuit theory, digital systems, and programming alongside hands-on lab work.

These days my focus is on analog systems, power distribution, diagnostics, and practical problem-solving with real hardware. I’m most comfortable behind an oscilloscope with a circuit on the bench, but I also have working experience in CAD and simulation tools such as AutoCAD, Fusion 360, Eagle, Multisim, and SPICE.

Projects and Skills

Nearly everything on this site was built from scratch as a personal learning project. My projects page highlights work that combines analog and digital design, from laser systems to embedded controllers and audio visualizers. I enjoy the challenge of creating complete working hardware and software stacks rather than stopping at theory.

My homelab is one of my proudest accomplishments: a multi-node Proxmox cluster running a full self-hosted service stack including a local large-language-model instance, all powered by reclaimed hardware. Building it entirely from spare parts and open-source tools taught me more about networking, systems administration, and distributed design than any class could.

Motivation

I became an engineer because I love making things work—whether that’s troubleshooting a power circuit or assembling a new machine from scrap. The more I learn, the more tools I gain to create. My long-term goal is to keep turning ideas into functioning devices and eventually run projects on a larger scale, both professionally and independently.

Beyond Engineering

I live on the north side of Indianapolis. Outside the lab, I’m into weightlifting, hiking, camping, cooking (I even wrote a cookbook), and leatherworking. Those hands-on hobbies keep me grounded and creative, and often influence the mechanical side of my engineering projects.

Looking Ahead

Right now I’m seeking opportunities where I can keep building—roles that value practical problem-solving, electronics expertise, and a willingness to learn quickly. I want to work with people who make real things, then come home and build more for fun.
